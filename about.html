VP, Product

@ Hearst Magazines

Oversaw product team structure and strategy across three org restructures, leading teams at both horizontal (discipline) and vertical (vertical) levels.

  • ▸2025–now: VP, Site Experience — leading teams across all Hearst Magazines brands for SEO, audience development, content experience and navigation/wayfinding.
  • ▸Launched AI-powered article summarization and text-to-speech across the Hearst Magazines portfolio.
  • ▸Building an agentic workflow on top of our internal design system so product and editorial teams can spin up branded pages and features without a front-end hand-off.
  • ▸Coded an internal analytics dashboard that pulls from the GitHub, Looker and our internal APIs to tie feature launches to audience impact — so we can see which shipped work actually moved the numbers.
  • ▸2023–2025: VP, Enthusiast & Wellness — ran product for the E&W brand vertical. Launched a new Masterclass template, new paywall experiences, and the platform features powering Oprah's Favorite Things.
  • ▸2021–2023: VP, Core — ran the Core disciplinary track with Directors and PMs across Content (replatformed the front-end for all Hearst US Magazines sites), Storytelling, CMS (new platform initiative), Search (replatformed to in-house Elasticsearch), and Video (relaunched in-house player).

2018–2021

Product Director

@ The Wall Street Journal

Ran the full product lifecycle for WSJ's suite of editorial tools and underlying services — strategy, discovery, ownership, and stakeholder relations across the newsroom and parent companies.

  • ▸Led discovery, development and global launch of a new content planning and workflow platform — first sprint to MVP in 4 months, global launch by year-end, unifying workflows previously split across several apps.
  • ▸Served as WSJ product stakeholder for consumer search — coordinating Dow Jones data teams, newsroom stakeholders, structured-data enhancements, related-article recommendations, and business requirements during a back-end service migration.
  • ▸Shepherded new additions to the WSJ corpus for search, including interactive graphics and user-focused features like searching on a story's print headline.
  • ▸Partnered with Amazon to bring a WSJ internal tool to the public for the 2020 Election — used custom taxonomies and NLP to turn millions of transcripts into a searchable database.
  • ▸Wrote business-logic requirements for internal tools sitting behind Elasticsearch APIs; built Kibana dashboards; stood up new ways of measuring internal processes.
  • ▸Product Owner for WSJ Newsletters; shepherded the portfolio through a vendor migration.
  • ▸Recruited and helped establish the first Program Management function for internal editorial publishing systems.
  • ▸Kicked off a design system to standardize UX and speed up tool construction across every team.
